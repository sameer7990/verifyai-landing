The Problem

Manual verification is costing your clinic every single day

The average audiology front desk spends 45+ minutes per day on hold with payers. That’s 4 hours a week, 200 hours a year — doing a job software should handle in seconds.

  • ⏱
    45+ minutes on hold daily
    Front desk staff call payer lines for basic eligibility — time that should go to patients, not hold music.
  • 🎧
    Hearing aid benefits are unusually complex
    Per ear or per pair? Every 24 months or 36? Payer reps give inconsistent answers. Patients get surprised at checkout.
  • 💬
    Patients get surprised at checkout
    Inconsistent payer rep answers lead to wrong quotes. When the bill is higher than expected, it erodes trust.
  • 📋
    No audit trail when patients dispute quotes
    When a patient says "you told me it was covered," there's nothing to fall back on. Verbal notes don't hold up.

How it works

From patient name to full benefit summary in 3 steps

No EHR integration required to get started. No payer contracts. No IT project. Your front desk is running verifications in under an hour.

Step 01
📋
Enter patient + payer info
Name, DOB, member ID, and payer. Takes 20 seconds. Or batch-upload tomorrow’s schedule the night before.

⚡
